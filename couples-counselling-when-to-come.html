On Couples

Couples Counselling: When to Come and What to Expect

For the couple who's been arguing about whether to go.

Most couples wait too long. By the time they walk into my office, the arguments have been circling for months — sometimes years.

When to Come

The short answer: before you think you need to.

The same argument keeps returning. You've stopped talking about the things that matter. Something happened — an affair, a betrayal, a secret. You're at a crossroads. Or you're just starting out and want to build something strong.

What Actually Happens

You sit together. I sit across from both of you. I'm not a referee. I'm watching what happens between you — how you speak, listen, defend, withdraw, or reach for each other.

Often the patterns become visible within minutes. My work is to slow this down and help you see what you're doing — not with blame, but with clarity.

It's Not Comfortable

Couples therapy is honest. It's confronting. It requires you both to look at your part — not just your partner's.

But it's also the place where couples discover they still have something worth fighting for. Not fighting with each other. Fighting for the relationship.

How Long Does It Take?

Some couples find clarity in six to eight sessions. Others work together over months. Sessions are 80 minutes — longer than individual therapy, because two people need more space.

If you're considering therapy — the first step is to come.
