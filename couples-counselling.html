When Couples Come to Therapy

Most couples wait too long. By the time they walk into my office, the arguments have been circling for months — sometimes years. The distance has calcified. One person has already half-left, emotionally. The other is exhausted from trying.

This is not a criticism. It makes sense. Asking for help with your relationship feels like admitting defeat. It isn't — but it feels that way.

You might recognise this

The same argument keeps returning. Different trigger, same dynamic. You fight about dishes, but it's never really about the dishes. It's about feeling unseen, unappreciated, controlled, or abandoned — and neither of you can name that clearly enough to break the cycle.

You've stopped talking about the things that matter. The relationship has become functional. You coordinate logistics — children, finances, schedules — but the intimacy has gone quiet.

Something happened. An affair. A betrayal of trust. A loss. A secret. And you don't know how to move forward together without pretending it didn't happen.

Or you're at a crossroads — thinking about separation, thinking about staying, unable to decide.

You don't need to be in crisis

Couples therapy can also benefit your relationship when you're courting and want to get more connected, when you're coming out to parents and family, when you're seriously thinking of a life together, when you're awaiting milestones like trying to have a child, or when you simply want to build something strong from the beginning. This is one of the best reasons to come.

Gestalt Couples Therapy

How Couples Therapy Works in My Practice

We sit together. Some people wonder what a couples counsellor actually does. Is she a referee? An advisor? I'm neither.

I observe and reflect what happens as you speak, how you listen, where you defend, where you shut down, where you reach toward each other and miss.

Often the patterns become visible: the way one partner begins a sentence and the other is already bracing. The moment someone softens and the other doesn't notice. The thing that gets said over and over without ever being heard.

My work is to notice, to listen, and to slow this down. You'll sometimes hear me say "slow the tape." I help you reflect on what is happening, to find clarity, and then to create space for something different to happen.

The therapeutic work includes observing couple interaction, accessing individuals in the couple, deliberate practice in communication, addressing recurring conflictual themes, overcoming gridlocks, finding shared values and meaning, and contactful silence and intimacy.

The critical ingredient is the openness and intention of both individuals to let connection happen in the session. As a therapist, my role is to actively facilitate this. What happens after that depends on how we channel this new learning in our daily lives.

Common Questions

Couples Counselling in Singapore — FAQ

What does a couples counsellor do?

A couples counsellor helps two people see what happens between them. I'm not a referee deciding who's right. I observe how you speak, listen, defend, and reach for each other, reflect those patterns back to you, and create space for something different to happen.

How much does couples counselling cost in Singapore?

A session is $370 for 80 minutes, available in-person in Singapore or online.

How long is a couples therapy session?

Couples sessions are 80 minutes. Couples work needs more room than individual therapy, so both partners have time to speak, be heard, and slow down the patterns between them.

What is Gestalt couples therapy?

Gestalt couples therapy focuses on what happens between partners in the present moment — contact, withdrawal, and the patterns that repeat. Rather than analysing the past alone, it works with the living interaction in the room as it unfolds.

Do we have to be in crisis to come?

No. Couples therapy helps when you're courting, moving in together, awaiting a milestone like a child, or simply want to build something strong from the beginning. You don't need to wait for a crisis.

Do you offer online couples counselling?

Yes. Sessions are available in-person in Singapore or online, so couples in different locations or with demanding schedules can still meet together.
