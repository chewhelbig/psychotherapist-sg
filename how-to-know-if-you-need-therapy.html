On Psychotherapy

How to Know If You Need Therapy (Not Just Self-Help)

When the books and podcasts stop working — what the signal means.

You've read the books. Listened to the podcasts. Maybe tried meditation apps, journalling prompts, breathing exercises.

Some of it helped — for a while. Then the same patterns returned.

This is not a failure of effort. It's a signal.

Self-Help Has Limits

Self-help is useful when the issue is informational. You didn't know something, and now you do. You learn a technique, you practise it, things shift.

But some difficulties aren't informational. They're relational. They live in the body, in old emotional patterns, in the space between what you know and what you can actually do differently. No book reaches there — because what's needed isn't knowledge. It's presence. Another person who can see what you can't see alone.

Signs It Might Be Time

You keep arriving at the same place. Different relationships, different jobs, different cities — same feeling.

You understand the problem intellectually but can't shift it. You know why you react the way you do. And yet — the same thing keeps happening.

Your coping strategies are getting louder. What started as a glass of wine to unwind has become a bottle.

You feel alone with something you can't name.

Your body is speaking. Insomnia, chronic tension, unexplained pain, digestive issues, skin flares.

What Therapy Offers That Self-Help Can't

A relationship. That's the core of it.

Therapy works because something happens in the presence of another person that cannot happen alone. Old patterns surface. Feelings that were frozen begin to move. You say things out loud that you've never said, and they land differently than they do inside your head.

This is not something an AI can replicate. Not something a book can offer. It requires a human being, paying attention, in real time, to you.

You Don't Need to Be in Crisis

Most people wait too long. You don't need to be falling apart to come to therapy. Some of the most meaningful work happens with people who simply sense that something isn't quite right — and want to understand it before it becomes a crisis.

If you're considering therapy — the first step is to come.
