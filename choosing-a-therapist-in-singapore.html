Choosing a Therapist in Singapore: What Actually Matters

Credentials, fit, the first session — written from the therapist's side.

Searching for a therapist in Singapore can feel overwhelming. Hundreds of practitioners, multiple modalities, different credentials, a wide range of fees. How do you choose?

Credentials Are a Floor, Not a Ceiling

Check that your therapist has proper training. In Singapore, look for membership in professional bodies — SAC, SPS, or APACS. A postgraduate qualification matters.

But credentials alone don't predict whether the work will be effective. What matters more is what happens between you.

The Therapeutic Relationship Matters Most

Research consistently shows that the single strongest predictor of therapeutic outcome is the quality of the therapeutic relationship.

How safe you feel, how understood you feel, whether you trust this person — matters more than which modality they practise.

You can't know this from a website. You have to sit in the room.

What to Pay Attention to in the First Session

Did you feel heard? Not agreed with — heard.

Did the therapist seem genuinely present? Or were they going through a checklist?

Did you leave feeling something — even if it was unsettling? A good first session doesn't always feel comfortable. It feels real.

Fees

Psychotherapy in Singapore ranges from $40 to $350 per session. A higher fee doesn't guarantee a better therapist. But very low fees sometimes indicate limited training or experience. Find someone within your means whose work you trust.

The Only Real Test

Go to the first session. The best way to find out if therapy with someone works for you is to experience it.
